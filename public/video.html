Oh well. Now you know how videos look!

Do you know how to install videos into your project? Well its just like how you do it with Images

The only difference is that you put your video (as an mp4) into your /video directory

Now, lets see how this video looks behind the scenes:

<video width="400" height="400" controls>
<source src="./video/WhoIAm.mp4" type="video/mp4">
Your Browser does not support the video tag.
</video>

Lets break it down!

The <video> tag is used to add videos into your website

Similar to the <img> tag, the video needs a height and width.

The <source tag specifies where the video is located, and what type of video it is. In this example, the video is called ./video/WhoIAm and the type is a mp4 file.

The message below this is just in case the video doesn't render. This happens if for some reason the browser doesn't understand the <video> tag. However, most browsers do.

And thats it!

Remember:

You video must be inside your /videos directory. If it isn't then there is a chance the video won't work.

Your video must be saved as a certain file. There are multiple ways to save a movie (ie mp4, ogg, etc.), and all you need to do is define the type of movie (see the example).
